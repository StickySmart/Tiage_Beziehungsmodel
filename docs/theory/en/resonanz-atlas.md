## The Tradition Lines

### India: Nada Brahma

**"Sound is Brahman"** — the oldest known formulation of the vibration principle (Upanishads, ca. 800–200 BCE).

- **Nada Brahma**: All of creation is sound/vibration; OM as primordial vibration
- **Yoga**: Physical practice for harmonizing inner vibrations
- **Ayurveda**: Health as balance of doshas (vibration qualities)

**Connection to Tiage model:** OSHO philosophy in the model is directly rooted in this Indian tradition.

---

### Antiquity: Heraclitus and Pythagoreans

**Heraclitus** (ca. 500 BCE): *"Everything flows"* — reality as process, not substance.

**Pythagoreans**: Numbers as vibration ratios. The harmony of the spheres: planetary movements follow musical proportions. Kinship of souls as harmonic resonance.

**Connection to Tiage model:** The dominance harmony in the model (complementary polar pairs = 100 points) has its deepest root here.

---

### Modern Philosophy: Hartmut Rosa — Resonance Theory

**Hartmut Rosa** (*Resonance: A Sociology of Our Relationship to the World*, 2016) is the first sociologist to formulate a systematic **resonance theory** for the 21st century.

Rosa distinguishes:
- **Resonance**: Being touched by and touching the world — a reciprocal relationship, not an echo
- **Alienation**: The opposite — contact without connection, activity without response

> *"Resonance does not mean agreeing or wanting the same things — but that one mutually touches the other."* — Hartmut Rosa

**Connection to Tiage model:** Rosa's resonance theory is the closest academic parallel to Tiage's resonance. Both say: resonance is not fusion, but an **answer-relationship** between two autonomous beings.

### Hermeticism: Law of Vibration

**The Hermetic Principle** (*Kybalion*, 1908; based on Hermes Trismegistus): *"Everything is Mind; the Universe is Mental."* Third Hermetic Law: **Law of Vibration** — nothing rests, everything vibrates.

**Alchemy**: Transmutation as change of vibration — lead to gold as metaphor for inner transformation.
